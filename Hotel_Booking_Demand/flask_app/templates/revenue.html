Revenue Insights & Summary
📈 Monthly ADR Trend

The monthly Average Daily Rate (ADR) illustrates seasonal pricing behavior throughout the year. Higher ADR values generally indicate periods of increased demand where hotels apply premium pricing strategies. Lower ADR values represent off-season periods with reduced occupancy.

Business Insight
  • Peak travel months contribute significantly to revenue.
  • Dynamic pricing can maximize profitability during high-demand seasons.
🏨 Revenue by Hotel Type

Revenue generated by City Hotels and Resort Hotels is compared to identify which hotel category contributes more to the overall business performance.

Business Insight
  • The hotel category with higher revenue should receive greater operational focus.
  • Pricing strategies may vary depending on hotel type and customer demand.
📊 ADR by Market Segment

Average Daily Rate varies across different market segments, reflecting differences in customer booking behavior and pricing opportunities.

Business Insight
  • High-value market segments generate better revenue.
  • Marketing campaigns can prioritize profitable customer segments.
📌 Revenue Summary
  • Revenue performance is strongly influenced by seasonal booking demand.
  • ADR remains one of the primary indicators of hotel profitability.
  • Customer segmentation provides opportunities for targeted pricing strategies.
  • Optimizing occupancy during low-demand periods can improve overall revenue.
  • Continuous monitoring of ADR trends supports better business decision-making.
